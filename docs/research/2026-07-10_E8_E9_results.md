## E8 — volatility-compression breakout (squeeze): **FAIL**

Rules (fixed a priori): BB(20, 2σ) inside Keltner(EMA20, 1.5×ATR20) for ≥5
sessions, then upward breakout → buy next open; exit close<EMA20 or 40 bars;
K=3; 29-ETF frozen universe; 5 bps/side; $1,000 NAV-compounded.

| Window | CAGR | %/mo | maxDD | Sharpe | n | win |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Gate 2000–2013** | **−1.43%** | −0.12% | 24.5% | −0.21 | 187 | 31.0% |
| Secondary 2014–26 | +1.10% | +0.09% | 12.6% | 0.24 | 151 | 38.4% |
| Full 2000–26 | −0.24% | −0.02% | 27.9% | −0.02 | 338 | 34.3% |

Gates: CAGR ≥15% **FAIL** (−1.43%); maxDD ≤60% pass (24.5%); n≥30 ok (187).
**E8 VERDICT: FAIL.** The breakout family loses money net of costs in the
hostile regime and earns ~1%/yr in a 12-year bull. A 31–38% win rate with no
tail winners large enough to carry it — compression predicts *expansion*,
not *direction*. Fourth family falsified; closed absent a new dated
decision.

## E9 — "never book a loss" deep-dip audit: **FAIL** (both predictions confirmed)

Rules (fixed a priori): buy broad-US/sector ETFs at close ≤ 0.80×ATH, sell
at +15%, NO stop, NO max hold, K=5, 5 bps/side, $1,000 NAV-compounded.

| Window | CAGR | %/mo | maxDD | Sharpe | n | win |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Gate 2000–2013** | **+3.46%** | 0.28% | 45.2% | 0.29 | 25 | **100%** |
| Secondary 2014–26 | +7.77% | 0.63% | 27.2% | 0.63 | 28 | **100%** |
| Full 2000–26 | +5.47% | 0.44% | 45.2% | 0.43 | 53 | **100%** |

Pre-registered predictions vs outcome:
1. *"The never-book-a-loss property will be (nearly) true in realized
   terms."* → **CONFIRMED: 0 of 53 closed trades realized a loss. 100% win
   rate.** The Reddit claim is literally true.
2. *"…but CAGR well under 15% with a large hidden unrealized tail."* →
   **CONFIRMED:** gate CAGR 3.46% (vs 15% bar); worst per-position
   unrealized drawdown **−79.7%**; longest hold to reach +15%: **4,289
   trading days ≈ 17 years** (a 2000-era entry that stayed underwater
   through two crashes); cash idle >50% of NAV on **38%** of all days.

Gates: CAGR ≥15% **FAIL** (3.46%); maxDD ≤60% pass (45.2%); n≥10 ok (25).
**E9 VERDICT: FAIL** for the high-return goal.

**The audit's point, now quantified:** the thread's most-upvoted strategy
("I have never booked losses") is *accurately described and still bad*. A
100% realized win rate coexists with ~3–5%/yr returns, a −80% unrealized
position, a 17-year underwater hold, and 38% dead capital — the win-rate
statistic measures bookkeeping, not performance. Dividends (excluded by the
program's price convention) would raise the CAGR a few points but change no
verdict and no mechanism.

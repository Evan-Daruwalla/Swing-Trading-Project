# E1 power calculation — IBS<0.20 signal frequency (2026-07-09)

**PRD task M1.6.** Purpose: determine whether E1 (ETF IBS mean reversion)
generates enough independent signals to reach statistical significance in a
useful timeframe — the council's binding concern (research brief 2026-07-08).

**INTEGRITY NOTE:** every number here is derived from IBS = (close−low)/
(high−low), a price-range statistic. **No post-signal returns were computed.**
Return measurement is off-limits until the pre-registration doc (M1.7) is
committed. Source: `swing.db` (89,647 valid bar-days after skipping XLRE's 19
zero-range bars). Reproduce: the M1.6 script logic on the frozen universe.

## Headline

- **Signal rate: 19.6%** of valid bar-days have IBS<0.20 (17,572 / 89,647).
- **~1,431 signal-days per year** across the 29-ETF universe.
- **73% of trading days** (2,299 of ~3,146) carry ≥1 signal; mean **7.6**
  simultaneous signals on a signal-day.
- **E1 is signal-abundant and capacity-constrained**, not signal-starved:
  the limit on trades is how many positions you hold at once, not how many
  signals fire.

## Capacity → trades/year

Signals available (~1,431/yr) far exceed what a small book can take, so the
constraint is concurrency. Modeled with an **assumed** average hold H (real
hold depends on the exit rule, pinned in M1.7 — not measured here):

| avg hold H | max slots K | realized trades/yr |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | 3 | 378 |
| 2 | 5 | 630 |
| 3 | 3 | 252 |
| 3 | 5 | 420 |
| 4 | 3 | 189 |
| 4 | 5 | 315 |

At K=5, H=3 (~420 realized trades/yr):

| pre-reg N (closed trades) | live paper time |

| 100 | ~2.9 months |
| 200 | ~5.7 months |
| 384 | ~11.0 months |

## Verdict: E1 IS POWERABLE

- **Backtest (M2):** 12.5 years × ~420 trades/yr capacity = **thousands of
  historical trades** — vastly more than enough to estimate expectancy and
  drawdown precisely. The backtest is not noise-limited.
- **Live paper (M3):** reaches **N=200 in ~6 months**, N=100 in ~3 months —
  a meaningful sample well within the project horizon.
- **Implication for M1.7 pre-registration:** a minimum-N kill criterion in
  the 100–200 closed-trade range is both statistically reasonable and
  reachable. The overlay (E2/veto) experiment reaches its own N far slower
  (only signals that pass to the treatment sleeve), so its readout horizon
  must be set separately and longer.

**HONEST CAVEAT:** frequency ≠ edge. High signal count means results *can*
be precise; it says NOTHING about whether the post-signal return is positive.
That is exactly what M2 measures — after M1.7 locks the rules.

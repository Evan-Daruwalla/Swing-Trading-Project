## Bake-off verdict (the pre-registered primary question)

**No new regime gate beats the plain 200-DMA overlay on the robust criterion
(maxDD cut ≥ 10 pp AND Sharpe ≥ buy-hold in BOTH windows).** Only gate (d),
the existing E6 200-DMA gate, satisfies the overlay criteria in both windows —
confirming E6/E7: plain trend-timing is the robust drawdown overlay, and none
of VIX term structure, credit spreads, or breadth improves on it across
regimes.

## The one result that must be reported honestly: VIX-TS clears PASS-RA

Per the pre-registered D1 tiers, **the VIX term-structure gate (a) is the
program's first PASS-RA** — gate-window (2006–13) Sharpe 0.80 (≥ 0.80 bar),
above SPY buy-hold in both windows, positive CAGR in both. By D1's rule a
PASS-RA becomes an M3 forward-paper candidate (it does **not** authorize live
capital). I'm recording it because the rule was fixed before the run — but
with the skepticism it deserves:

- **It is a fragile pass.** The 2006–13 gate window contains exactly **one**
  crash (2008) — VIX3M history starts 2006, so this arm misses the 2000–02
  dot-com bear entirely (disclosed in the prereg). A gate that goes to cash on
  high near-term vol will always look good in a short sample dominated by the
  one event it dodged. The pass rests largely on **dodging 2008**.
- **It fails its own overlay test in the other regime.** In 2014→ the VIX gate
  had a **worse** drawdown than buy-hold (44.1% vs 35.6%) — it whipsawed in
  the low-vol bull. Its secondary Sharpe (0.79) only barely edges SPY (0.74).
- So it clears the pre-registered risk-adjusted bar, but it is **not robust
  and not a return engine** — one crash-dodge in a truncated window plus a
  marginal bull-era Sharpe. Honest label: a **weak PASS-RA**, worth forward
  paper alongside E6-1× and nothing more.

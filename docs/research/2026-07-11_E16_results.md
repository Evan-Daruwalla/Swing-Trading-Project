# E16 results — cross-sectional weekly reversal

**2026-07-11 (CST) · per prereg `a090294` (doc-only, committed before the
runner). Runner `scripts/run_e16_weekly_reversal.py`; 39 survivor large-caps
from .e8e9_cache; swing.db untouched; frozen tripwire GREEN. No parameter
changed after results.**

Each week-end, rank the 39 by trailing 5-session return, buy the bottom 4
(biggest losers) at next open, full weekly rebalance, 5 bps/side. 7,616
entries over 1,905 week-ends.

| Window | E16 CAGR | maxDD | Sharpe | EW-39 | SPY |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Gate 2000–2013** | **16.76%** | **65.9%** | 0.61 | −0.47% | 1.72% / 0.19 |
| Secondary 2014–26 | 10.68% | 64.0% | 0.53 | 13.97% | 11.98% / 0.74 |
| Full 2000–26 | 13.86% | 65.9% | 0.57 | 6.06% | 6.40% |

**D1 verdict: FAIL (clean).**
- **PASS-HR fails** — and this is the notable part: the gate CAGR **16.76%
  clears the 15% return bar** (the *only* experiment in 16 to do so), but the
  **65.9% maxDD breaches the 60% ceiling**, and the secondary CAGR (10.68%)
  is under 15%. Fails on drawdown and on the secondary window.
- **PASS-RA fails** — gate Sharpe 0.61 < 0.80, and loses SPY's Sharpe in 2014→.

**Why the headline number is not a discovery — the survivorship trap, exactly
as pre-registered.** Reversal / dip-buying is the strategy *most* flattered by
survivorship bias: buying the biggest losers only works if the losers come
back, and this universe is 39 companies that are all still alive today (no
Lehman, no Enron). Every dip in it eventually recovered *by construction*.
So a high CAGR from dip-buying survivors is the expected artifact, not
evidence of edge — which is precisely why the prereg fixed the
asymmetric-falsification framing (only a FAIL is clean) before running. The
result is a FAIL (drawdown + Sharpe), so it is interpretable: even with
survivorship handing it a 16.76% headline, weekly reversal could not clear
the risk-controlled bar — it carried a **66% drawdown** and a sub-0.80 Sharpe.
The prior counter-evidence held too (our fill-timing ablation): the next-open
long-only construct is exactly the lossy version of the paper's close-based
edge.

**Not tuned.** The obvious "fix" (add a stop to cut the 66% DD) is exactly the
tuning-a-FAIL move the discipline forbids; not done. **Weekly reversal
closed.** Program 0/17 primary; 0/4 D1-tiered. Noted: E16 is the closest any
experiment came to the return bar, and it took maximal survivorship flattery +
a blown risk ceiling to get there.

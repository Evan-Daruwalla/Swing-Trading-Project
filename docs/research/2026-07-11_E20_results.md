**The nuanced finding:** the ex-date effect is *real but sub-scale* — the
price drop on the ex-date is on average ~10 bps *less* than the dividend
(net of costs), a small positive per-trade edge (57.7% win). But it does not
make a strategy: captures are ~90/yr, one session each, so the edge barely
touches the capital and compounds to 0.62%/yr in the gate window and turns
**negative post-2014** (−1.15%) — another micro-effect that decayed. And this
is **pre-tax**: dividends are taxed as ordinary income, which (disclosed in
the prereg) would flip the +0.10%/trade gross edge negative for a real retail
account. **Dividend capture closed** — the folklore edge exists, is tiny,
decayed, and is eaten by tax. Program 0 PASS-HR / 1 weak PASS-RA / 19
attempts.
